PERSONAL STATEMENT

Since I was young, I've always enjoyed playing with numbers and found myself keen on technology. Most of my family admired my brilliance but were continually irritated by how I disassembled most of their appliances while attempting to figure out "the physics" behind them. However, their continuous comments that "that is not a Rwandan girl's behavior" drove me to want to learn more.

My desire to prove to them that girls/women can produce effective technologies drove me to work hard and learn more about technology. As such, since I couldn't access all the electronics I wanted, I sought small gigs at street movie vendors and bought my first phone at 14, which was my first proud non-academic accomplishment.

So far as I know, my life has been filled with much success and learning. Despite a challenging first high school year at a competitive international school in a foreign country, I received several prizes and opportunities. In this vibrant community, I developed many skills such project management, communication, public speaking, and leadership skills, all gained from multiple opportunities that equipped me with more leadership skills.

However, owing to the quantity of plastic garbage that appalled me at the beaches, polluted streets, and so on, I began to love the environment and sustainable techniques to safeguard our world throughout my foreign stay. As a result of my expertise in technology, my future goal is to devise appropriate and easy sustainability tech solutions to the various challenges facing our world. I am currently seeking IT employment in conservation-related organizations and working on projects of this nature.

Throughout my journey, I have evolved into an aspiring computer engineer interested in hardware development, assessing computer-related impacts on society, and aiming to work with African Tech firms for growth and development.
